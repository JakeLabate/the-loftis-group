The method

Every engagement starts with one question.

What three things do you want your organization to be known for? Answer that, and every channel, campaign, and conversation has a job to do. Here is how Tony answers it for his own practice.

Message 01

Messaging the whole organization can carry

Messaging frameworks and strategic communications that align staff, board, and supporters, so the story stays consistent no matter who is telling it. Fundraising guidance included, because the case for support is a message too.

Message 02

Stories planned before they are gathered

Storytelling and content creation that starts with the end in mind: decide which stories you need, then collect the testimonials and material that prove them. Written and edited with a working writer's discipline.

Message 03

Digital that fits a small team

Social media strategy, content calendars, and practical AI workflows sized for organizations where communications is one slice of somebody's job. Measurable results, not busywork.

Why storytelling

Tony learned what a clear message and a mobilized community can do, in the hardest way a parent can.

In 2011, his 13-year-old daughter went missing. For 12 days the family ran the full communications playbook: a clear message, social media, and the press, until a tip brought her home. Tony turned that experience into Find Your Missing Child, a nonprofit that taught other parents to do the same. The work was covered by The Boston Globe and NPR.
