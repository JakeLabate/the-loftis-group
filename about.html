About

Tony Loftis

Communications strategist. Award-winning writer. Nonprofit leader.

  • Runs The Loftis Group, a communications consultancy for small nonprofits.
  • Decades of communications work, from the US Department of Transportation to the City of Cambridge.

The through line

Tony Loftis runs The Loftis Group, a communications consultancy for small nonprofits. For most of his career he has solved communications problems for organizations of every size. The list runs from the US Department of Transportation and the City of Cambridge to social justice nonprofits across Greater Boston.

His agency years included the IBM Global Technology Services account at Davis Murphy Group, then the firm's second-largest by revenue. Later, communications strategy at fama PR. As communications director at Demand Abolition, he helped drive a campaign working to end the illegal commercial sex industry.

The story that changed the work

In November 2011, Tony's 13-year-old daughter ran away. For 12 days, Tony and his wife did the one thing they knew: they communicated. A clear message. A Facebook page shared by thousands of strangers. Press coverage. A tip came in, and she came home safe.

Tony turned the experience into Find Your Missing Child. The nonprofit taught parents to use social media and the press to bring their kids home, starting with a step-by-step guide for families. The Boston Globe and NPR covered the work. Tony wrote for HuffPost on runaways and the missing persons community.

Teaching runs through all of it

Tony has taught social media to youth mentoring groups, theater companies, anti-sex-trafficking organizations, and the missing persons community. Same goal every time: give small teams tools they can actually use. He has also written about what four years of improv classes at ImprovBoston taught him about communications.

Civic leadership

Off the clock, Tony has led across Massachusetts youth soccer. As president of the Boston Area Youth Soccer League, the largest in the state, he helped put 18,000 players from 53 towns on the field. He strengthened the organization's institutional controls and led a governance reform.

He is a 2014 graduate of the MetroWest Leadership Academy. Past boards include Habitat for Humanity's MetroWest affiliate, Wayland Youth Soccer, CASCAP, and the MetroWest Nonprofit Network. He also volunteers at professional golf tournaments, including as a transportation captain for the PGA Tour's Boston stop.

Now

Today Tony serves on three boards. The MassBay Community College Foundation, which raises funds and awards scholarships for MassBay students. The John and Alice Coltrane Home. The Institute for Storycraft and Information Gathering.

He holds a B.A. from Tufts University and studied at John Jay College of Criminal Justice.
