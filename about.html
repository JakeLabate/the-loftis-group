About

Tony Loftis

Communications strategist. Award-winning writer. Nonprofit leader.

The through line

Tony Loftis runs The Loftis Group, a communications consultancy for small nonprofits. For most of his career he has solved communications problems for organizations of every size, from the US Department of Transportation and the City of Cambridge to social justice nonprofits across Greater Boston.

His agency years included leading the IBM Global Technology Services account at Davis Murphy Group, then the firm's second-largest account by revenue, and later work as a communications strategist at fama PR. As communications director at Demand Abolition, he helped drive an advocacy campaign working to end the illegal commercial sex industry.

The story that changed the work

In November 2011, Tony's 13-year-old daughter ran away from home. For 12 days, Tony and his wife did the one thing they knew how to do: they communicated. A clear message, a Facebook page shared by thousands of strangers, and press coverage produced the tip that brought her home safely.

Tony took what the family learned and founded Find Your Missing Child, a nonprofit that taught parents to use social media and the press to bring their kids home, starting with a step-by-step social media guide for families. The organization's work was covered by The Boston Globe and NPR, and Tony wrote regularly for HuffPost on runaways, family abduction, and the missing persons community.

Teaching runs through all of it

Tony has taught social media skills to youth mentoring groups, theater companies, anti-sex-trafficking organizations, and the missing persons community, always with the same goal: give small teams tools they can actually use. He has also written about what four years of improv classes at ImprovBoston taught him about communications.

Civic leadership

Off the clock, Tony has held leadership roles across Massachusetts youth soccer, including serving as president of the Boston Area Youth Soccer League, the largest soccer league in the state, which puts 18,000 players from 53 towns in eastern Massachusetts on the field. In that role he strengthened the organization's institutional controls and led a reform of its governance.

A 2014 graduate of the MetroWest Leadership Academy, he has also served on boards for Habitat for Humanity's MetroWest affiliate, Wayland Youth Soccer, CASCAP, and the MetroWest Nonprofit Network. In his spare time he volunteers at professional golf tournaments, including serving as a transportation captain for the PGA Tour's Boston stop.
