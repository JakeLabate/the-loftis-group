Services

The work, in plain terms.

Strategic communications and fundraising guidance for small nonprofits, built around storytelling, digital strategy, and measurable results.

Service 01

Messaging and strategic communications

Most small nonprofits do not have a message problem. They have a consistency problem. The Loftis Group builds messaging frameworks that resonate with external and internal audiences, so everyone from the executive director to the newest volunteer tells the same story on every channel.

That includes fundraising communications. The case for support, the appeal, and the thank-you note are all part of one message, and they should sound like it.

Service 02

Storytelling and content creation

Testimonials gathered without a plan produce a hard drive full of footage and no story. Tony starts with the questions that matter: What three things do you want your organization to be known for? Which narratives prove them? Then the interviews, the writing, and the editing all pull in the same direction.

Outline first. Then welcome the unexpected discoveries you find along the way.

Service 03

Social media strategy

Channel plans, content calendars, and posting rhythms matched to the capacity of a small team. Tony has taught social media skills to youth mentoring groups, theater companies, anti-sex-trafficking organizations, and the missing persons community.

The bar is always the same: show up consistently, sound like yourself, and measure what matters.

Service 04

Practical AI for small teams

AI will not replace your organization's voice, and it should not. Used well, it makes first drafts faster and editing sharper. Tony helps teams build honest AI workflows: draft with more than one model, prompt with specifics, fact-check everything, and edit until the output sounds like you.

An investment in learning the tools now pays off every week after.

Who this is for

Built for teams where communications is one slice of a bigger job.

Most of Tony's clients are not communications professionals. They run programs, raise money, and manage everything else, with communications wedged in between. The engagement is designed for that reality: clear recommendations, plain language, and systems a small team can actually run.
